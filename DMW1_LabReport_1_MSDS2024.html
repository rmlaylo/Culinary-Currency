ABSTRACT

This report explores the potential cost implications, taking into account the scarcity of ingredients in the Philippines, for a range of recipes representing different cuisines and the particular components required to prepare them. It examines the viability of preparing these cuisines in the Philippines considering their costs, or from another standpoint, identifies which cuisines and recipes can be produced based on ingredient availability, with import data serving as a measure of scarcity. The group selected this topic due to the fluctuations in commodity prices, particularly in the food sector, stemming from macroeconomic factors, notably inflation. Reviewing a dataset that covers pandemic to post-pandemic import transactions will reflect the movement of commodities in the short-term. Given that global trade is dynamic and difficult to predict, this heavily impacts the culinary industry in terms of the availability and affordability of imported ingredients. In the Philippines which is widely known for vibrant culinary traditions, the group aims to answer the question: Which global cuisine's ingredients is the most costly to impost to the Philippines?

For this analysis, the group utilized two primary datasets: (1) The PH Customs Import Datasets, encompassing all imported goods from various countries, and (2) a Recipe database featuring diverse recipes from various cuisines, along with the corresponding ingredients required for their preparation. By linking the ingredients with data related to imported goods, the group made the decision to focus on the top 20 ingredients for each cuisine. This choice was based on the fact that 50% of all recipes within each cuisine category incorporated these top 20 ingredients. While validating and consolidating the top ingredients, the group has also started cleaning the datasets using varying strenuous methods to confidently start using the data for analyses.

The Problem Statement

In the dynamic landscape of global trade, the culinary industry is deeply affected by the availability and affordability of ingredients sources from different countries of the world. In the context of the Philippines specifically, a country known for its diverse and vibrant culinary traditions, an intriguing question arises:

Which global cuisine's ingredients is the most costly to impost to the Philippines?

For those deeply involved in the culinary industry, such as chefs, restaurant owners, and food distributors, insights gathered from answering this problem statement can enable them to optimize their procurement processes and manage costs effectively. For cost-intensive cuisines specifically, this can even kick-start innovation efforts within the local culinary fusion scene to promote development of dishes from global cuisines with a twist: by capitalizing on locally sourced ingredients as a substitute and in turn contribute to the sustainability of producing these cuisines in the Philippines.

D. Creation of Consolidated Table

Create dictionary linking ingredient and HSCODE

A link or common key must be created in order to unify the two separate datasets before conducting the analysis. The method to be used would utilize to conditional logics:

  1. Check if entries under HSCODE is the appropriate classification ID for the ingredient
  2. Check if entries under GOODSDESCRIPTION contain the ingredient

Two conditions must be used to take into consideration the following instances:

  1. Entries sharing the same HSCODE but refers to different ingredients (e.g. lemon juice and lime juice)
  2. Entries with GOODSDESCRIPTION that contains the ingredient but do not refer to the ingredient itself (e.g. orange referring to orange-colored items)

A dictionary with the following characteristics was initially created to create key-value pairs between the ingredient, HSCODE, and search word for GOODSDESCRIPTION.

  • key: ingredient from the recipe_ingredients table
  • first value: list of HSCODE that corresponds to ingredient
  • second value: keyword or search word to be used in GOODSDESCRIPTION that corresponds to ingredient

The methodology for populating the dictionary involves the following steps:

  1. Check the top HSCODE that would appear when the ingredient was searched in the GOODSDESCRIPTION column.
  2. Investigate each HSCODE to determine the appropriate identifier/s per ingredient.
  3. Include all appropriate HSCODE in list as value of dictionary.

Create Consolidated table

The final table to create is the consolidated table that will link the customs_imports, recipe_ingredients and ingredients_hscode together and will be utilized heavily for exploratory data analysis.

First, another table is created that will utilize the ingredients_HSCODE table and determine all row entries found in customs_imports that satisfy these two conditions:

  1. HSCODE in both tables match with each other
  2. The regex_keyword in ingredients_HSCODE is contained in the GOODSDESCRIPTION column from the customs_imports table

After which, each row entry that satisfies those two conditions are joined to the corresponding ingredients_HSCODE entry.

        SELECT
